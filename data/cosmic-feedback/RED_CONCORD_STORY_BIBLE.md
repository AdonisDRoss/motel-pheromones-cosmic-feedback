# Red Concord Story Bible — Cosmic Feedback

## The galaxy's center: Nova
Nova is the capital city-world at the heart of the inner systems.
Every trade route, supply gate, and transit lane runs through it.
The Red Concord controls Nova. Controlling Nova means controlling everything that feeds the galaxy.

## The Astro Van
Donny's Astro Van is his ship on wheels — retrofitted for road and low-orbit travel.
It doubles as a home, a hideout, and the only vehicle Riff can reliably fix.
It is parked below Donny's office on Dust-9 at the start of the game.

## The Meridian War
The Meridian War began as a trade and supply conflict between the core systems and the neglected outer worlds.
The old alliances fractured when food, fuel, and safe transit started failing.
Red Concord rose as a military-economic coalition promising to keep the lanes open.
They won by controlling the roads between worlds: gates, refuel stations, relay towers, toll lanes, prison transports, supply depots.
After victory, they renamed occupation as peacekeeping, debt as citizenship, obedience as harmony.

## Red Concord: public version vs truth
**Public:** A stabilizing authority that saved civilization. Rationing, checkpoints, and patrol law are necessary to prevent collapse.

**Truth:** They turned emergency wartime control into permanent empire.
They control: food permits, fuel allotments, air filters, medicine, road movement, planetary debt, family work contracts, trade licenses, prison labor routes.

## Oppression after the war
The outer rim became a managed zone. Worlds like Dust-9 got low priority, high debt, and permanent checkpoint control.
Red Concord oppression looks like: patrol hoverbikes, turret gates, medicine withheld for unpaid debt, families recorded as labor collateral, dissidents marked as cargo.

## Dust-9: the starting world
A small, forgotten world at the edge of nothing. Too small to matter — which is why it is dangerous.
Donny disappeared here four years ago. Riff keeps the lights on.
Something buried under Dust-9 is producing a signal: the Cosmic Feedback.
Dex finds proof. Riff intercepts the message. Donny hears it before Red Concord can bury it.

## The characters
- **Donny** — freelance fixer, front man, lives in the Astro Van on Dust-9
- **Riff** — Donny's robot companion, fixes the van, intercepts signals
- **Dex** — off-world contact, sends the warning signal
- **D** — unknown. Shows up later.

## The Dex message (full text)
"Donny...
This is not a storm. It is feedback.
It starts under Dust-9.
Red Concord knows.
Nova is listening.
Bring the van.
Trust Riff.
Do not answer the return signal."

## Why it matters
The Cosmic Feedback is a signal leaking from something buried under Dust-9.
The Red Concord knows. Nova is watching.
Donny and Riff have to reach it before it gets buried again — or before them.
